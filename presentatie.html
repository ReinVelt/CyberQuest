Episode I
OPERATION ZERFALL

It is February 9, 2026. Compascuum, Drenthe, Netherlands. Ryan Weylant — software developer, radio amateur, and reluctant hero — is making an espresso.

He does not yet know that a USB stick has been taped under a stone bench at the medieval Ter Apel Klooster across the province. He does not know about Eva Weber, or Project Echo, or the eight people who died when hospital equipment stopped working at the wrong moment.

He knows about coffee.

Two thousand kilometres away, at a classified military R&D compound in the Steckerdoser Heide — a dark forest in Lower Saxony, Germany — Commander Dmitri Volkov is running the final calibration of a weaponised radio frequency system. Project Echo. Capable of disrupting any electronics within range. Hospitals. Power grids. Aircraft.

Three months ago, Project Echo killed Marlies Bakker, 67, grandmother of four. Her cardiac monitor failed mid-surgery. The cover story: equipment malfunction. Nobody looked twice.

Eva Weber looked twice. And she remembered a hacker in a farmhouse in Drenthe who once asked very good questions about SDR antennas.

This is the story of what happened next.

This is CyberQuest: Operation ZERFALL.
